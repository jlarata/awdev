Skills & Projects

Having worked for so many years teaching literature, I'll say that my area of expertise involves mainly humanistic studies, communication, listening and sharing ideas, and working with groups of people.
However, during the last few years I began to study and carry out small programming and web design projects. Below are some of them.

Film Society App

React + JS | Python + Flask (marsmallow, sqlalchemy, dotenv...) | MySQL

I am passionate about cinema. For ten years I have met with friends once a week, we have a delicious dinner, we watch a film, we discuss it and we keep a record on a spreadsheet (with more than four hundred films that we've shared all this years). For the tenth anniversary I developed a full-stack application so that my friends can use all this information in an accessible and intuitive way.

pAIdeia in the Rye

HTML + CSS + JS | ChatGPT + Craiyon + TTSMaker | Olive

One of my students' favorite novels is The Catcher in the Rye. My task usually consists of teaching them the literary strategies and techniques that J. D. Salinger used to write that text. In 2023 I proposed a project that we presented at the First Arts Day of Paideia School. My students "competed" with chatGPT to create an apocryphal fragment of the novel, which then I assembled in a classroom read out loud by TTS software along with an artistic video that I had edited with images from that time. Finally I also developed a simple website with a carousel to access the texts and information. I used the AI ​​application Craiyon to create some allusive images to decorate the web
